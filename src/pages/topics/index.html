OTDP and TLS Switching

Authentication protocols generally assume that clients (s.a. computers and smartpones) are secured. We argue that malwares can corrupt clients (32% of computers are infected by a malware ) and modify their expected behavior. In order to bring security to the client-side, we delegate sensitives operations (s.a. passwords inputs and signatures computations) to an external device assumed incorruptible, a trusted device.

As part of my training, I made two internships (3 and 5 months) at the University of Oslo on the OffPAD project , proposed by the OffPAD consortium , resulting of 6 demonstrators presented at the ACM CCS 2016 , A* conference. We also proposed a new trusted device architecture (OTDP) and a 3-party usage of TLS (TLS Switching) in a publication at SiOT (ESORICS Workshop) .
